Covid-19 in Italy
I. Cases
- The first known case of COVID-19 in the world was recorded on November 17, 2019, in Hubei Province, China. This case involved a 55-year-old man.
- The first case of COVID-19 in Italy was confirmed on January 31, 2020. It involved two Chinese tourists who were in Rome.
- The total number of confirmed COVID-19 cases in Italy exceeds 26.7 million.
- The situation is closely monitored, and updates on the distribution of cases by region and healthcare resource burden are regularly published here.

II. Mortality
- By the beginning of June 2024, nearly 0.2 million people had died from COVID-19 in Italy.
- The mortality rate among those diagnosed with COVID-19 is approximately 0.7%.
- COVID-19 related deaths are unevenly distributed across the country, with some regions, such as Lombardy, experiencing higher mortality rates compared to other regions.
- More detailed information about COVID-19 mortality in Italy is presented in the charts below.

III. Healthcare
- The Italian healthcare system faced enormous challenges due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Hospitals, especially in the northern part of the country, were overwhelmed, and medical resources, such as ventilators and intensive care beds, were severely limited.
- To alleviate the burden on the healthcare system, Italy implemented a series of countermeasures, including the construction of temporary field hospitals, recruitment of additional medical staff, and increased production and import of medical equipment.
- The COVID-19 vaccination campaign began at the end of 2020. By June 2024, the majority of the population had been vaccinated, significantly reducing the number of hospitalizations and deaths.
